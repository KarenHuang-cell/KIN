INTRO BODY:
  KIN partnered with France's Hurricane Group to bring the internationally
  acclaimed FISE (Festival International des Sports Extrêmes) to Singapore
  for the first time. Held on 22–23 November 2025 at *SCAPE and Somerset
  Skatepark, the event formed part of the Singapore Urban Sports and
  Fitness Festival — with KIN stepping up as co-organiser and promoter.

  This wasn't just a delivery job. KIN took on ownership and creative
  control, drawing on its deep event management experience to coordinate
  across continents, drive marketing strategy, and create meaningful youth
  community engagement. The result: an estimated 5,000 attendees and over
  SGD 500K in total PR value.

BODY:
  ### Cross-Border Collaboration
  KIN implemented a structured approach, conducting bi-weekly work-in-
  progress meetings with their French counterparts to ensure alignment
  throughout the planning process. The international collaboration, while
  initially challenging due to different working procedures, strengthened
  through open communication and post-event debriefs. Five days were
  dedicated to setup and two days to teardown, ensuring every aspect met
  international standards.

  ### The Fan Experience
  The event was designed to captivate both dedicated enthusiasts and curious
  newcomers, featuring high-level demonstrations by world-renowned athletes
  and transforming *SCAPE and Somerset Skatepark into a vibrant showcase of
  extreme sports talent and culture. This dual focus on world-class
  entertainment and grassroots participation created an inclusive atmosphere
  that resonated with urban sports enthusiasts and youth.

  ### Marketing and Media
  KIN deployed a comprehensive marketing approach leveraging social media
  and strategic partnerships. Media highlights included a feature in The
  Straits Times and a broadcast segment on Money FM 89.3 worth over
  SGD 100K, plus online coverage across 14 publications generating more
  than SGD 150K in PR value — with features in AsiaOne, Time Out Singapore,
  and Expat Living. Reach extended beyond Singapore into Malaysian outlets
  including The Star and Sinar Daily. Social media accounted for 48.5% of
  total PR value across 14 posts.

  ### Community Impact
  Beyond the spectacle of world-class athletic performances, the event
  served a larger purpose in promoting urban sports within Singapore's
  cultural landscape and creating pathways for aspiring athletes to connect
  with the broader urban sports movement.

  ### Looking Forward
  As the inaugural edition, FISE Singapore 2025 lays important groundwork
  for future iterations. Significantly, it showcases KIN's agility in
  transforming from event delivery partner to co-organiser and promoter —
  proving capability not just in operational excellence but in strategic
  vision, marketing acumen, and building international partnerships.
